About The Game
  • • Almost an exact copy of the nostalgic Balls built into old Windows systems back in the 90s. Many people will probably remember the working days of the time when the first super-fast IBM-s appeared at work! For hours on end, workers sat at newfangled computers playing games, and the most famous and popular game at that time was Lines or, as many people called it, Balls.
  • • What was good about the built-in Lines 98(Lines 98), and the fact that it worked equally well on any computer with an operating system from Microsoft. Lines practically do not need computer resources - the game is simple and ingenious. For those who missed the beginning of the computer era, here are the rules of the game.
  • • Balls of different colors appear on the playing field. The player must arrange them in such a way that horizontally, diagonally or vertically chains of five or more identical balls are formed. This one-color group disappears from the field. Keep in mind that the ball will not be able to pass to the selected position if the path there is blocked by others.

How to play
  • • Arrange five or more balls of the same color into one line.
  • • The line can be horizontal, vertical or diagonal.
  • • Touch and hold the ball and move to the cell that you want.
